    Pest Name: Thistle Caterpillar

    Information

    The thistle caterpillar, Vanessa cardui, is in the Nymphalidae family and order Lepidoptera. The orange-brown wings of an adult can span up to 2-2 7/8 inches. A body's typical length is one inch. Their forewings have a white bar and a black patch on the leading edge, while their hindwings have a row of five tiny black spots. There are four eyespots with a black, brown, and grey pattern on the undersides of the wings.


    Symptoms:

    1. When caterpillars hatch, they bind leaves with silk to make webs, giving them a sheltered space to feed.

    2. The soybean plant's upper canopy is defoliated due to caterpillars feeding on leaves.

    3. The caterpillars develop into a chrysalis hanging from the host plant after two to six weeks. The butterfly appears seven to ten days after hatching.

    Organic Control:

    Eggs and larvae can be treated using bioinsecticides based on Nuclear Polyhedrosis Virus (NPV) or Bacillus thuringiensis. In the evening, rice bran, molasses, or brown sugar-based bait solutions can be sprayed on the ground. Neem leaves or kernel extracts, as well as extracts from Pongamia glabra seeds, are mighty against Spodoptera litura larvae. For instance, NSKE 5 per cent or azadirachtin 1500 ppm (5 ml/l) can be applied during the egg stage to stop the egg from hatching.
