    Pest Name: Mosquito

    Information

    Mosquitoes belong to the same group as the true flies, Diptera. As such, they have a single pair of wings. Their usual features include long, thin legs and a head with a noticeable proboscis. Frequently, mosquito bodies and wings are covered in minute scales. Sizes in adults can range from 3 to 9 mm. In their gregarious phase, mosquitoes form enormous migratory swarms that travel hundreds or even thousands of miles on the wind or by flying. Crops in an infected area may be completely destroyed by these swarms.


    Symptoms:

    1. Young leaves, fragile items, and buds are all sources of sap for adults and nymphs. They use their needle-like rostrum to prick the plant tissues and inject poisonous saliva.

    2. Reddish-brown patches are the result of punctures. Shoots dry up, leaves curl and are severely malformed.



    Organic Control:

    For instance, azadirachtin kills mosquitoes by impairing hormone synthesis. Neem oil effectively eradicates various pests and diseases in the landscape due to its antibacterial qualities. It can be sprayed on herbs and vegetables up to the day of harvest and is safe to use on both ornamental and edible crops.
