    Pest Name: Locust

    Information

    Locusts are short-horned grasshoppers that become gregarious at higher population densities. Grasshoppers are plant-eating insects with limited mobility. Locusts can fly, nevertheless, and can travel great distances. The behaviour of grasshoppers and locusts is what differentiates them most.


    Symptoms:

    1. The type of plant and the locust's hunger affect the symptoms differently. Locusts primarily consume leaves, causing damage to the margins or removing significant amounts of the leaf.

    2. The insect may also attack fruits, seed pods, and grains. They may even eat stems and bark if they are starving. Additionally, they chew on the shoots, which separates the reproductive components.



    Organic Control:

    Encouraging naturally occurring biological control agents like wasps, web-spinning spiders, parasitic flies and worms, ants, birds, and frogs is essential. To lower the population density of larvae, entomopathogenic fungi and Metarhizium acridum could potentially be utilised. Utilize homemade rice bran and saltwater poison baits.
