    Pest Name: Spider Mites

    Information

    Spider mites are members of the Tetranychidae family, which includes about 1,200 species. They belong to the Acari subclass. Spider mites typically reside on the undersides of plant leaves, where they may weave protective silk webs. They can harm a plant by feeding by puncturing its cells.


    Symptoms:

    1. The feeding spider mites produce white to yellow specks on the upper surface of the leaves. As the infestation worsens, leaves initially look bronzed or silvery, then they get brittle, rip open between the veins, and eventually fall off.

    2. On the undersides of leaves, you can find spider mite eggs. Nestled in a cocoon resembling webbing, the spider mite itself can be found there. The spider mites will weave a web over infected plants.

    3. Side shoots can begin to grow when shoot tips become barren. Fruits suffer reductions in both quantity and quality when there is severe damage.



    Organic Control:

    Wash mites off the leaves and eliminate them if there is a slight infestation. Spray leaves extensively with solutions based on rapeseed, basil, soybean, and neem oils to lower T. urticae populations. To reduce the number of people, you can also use insecticidal soap solutions, nettle slurry, or garlic tea. Use predatory mites (like Phytoseiulus persimilis) or the biological insecticide Bacillus thuringiensis for host-specific biological control in fields. After the initial treatment, two to three days later, a second spray treatment application is required.
