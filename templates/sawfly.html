    Pest Name: Saw-fly

    Information

    Sawflies get their name from the saw-like appendage at the tip of their body. The "saw" that female sawflies use to implant eggs into leaves. Despite not having stingers, they are more closely related to wasps than flies. Although adult sawflies are rarely seen, on occasion, you could spot them around flowers or flower buds where their larvae harm the foliage.


    Symptoms:

    1. The larva initially eats leaves, but subsequently transitions to feeding on the edges of the midrib.

    2. The grubs' insatiable feeding caused them to produce countless shot holes and even riddle the entire leaves.

    3. They eat the shoot's epidermis, which causes seedlings to dry out and older plants to stop producing seeds.



    Organic Control:

    Applying irrigation at the seedling stage is essential for managing sawflies because most larvae perish from drowning. Severe cold lowers pest load—anti-feedant use of bitter gourd seed oil emulsion. Spray the crop with 25 EC quinolphos and 50 EC malathion at 1000 ml per hectare. This must be applied in roughly 600–700 liters of water per hectare.
