    Pest Name: Stem Borer

    Information

    During the vegetative stage, the stem borer larvae bore at the base of the plants. On more mature plants, they pierce the top nodes and feed downward. The yellow stem borer harms deepwater rice. It is found in aquatic environments where there is continuous flooding. Rice is severely harmed by the stem borer (Chilo polychrysus), a dangerous pest bug. Wheat, sugarcane, and maize are all infected as well.


    Symptoms:

    1. During the vegetative stage, damage at the base or along the central system can result in dead tillers, also known as "dead hearts," and during the reproductive phase, whitish, empty panicles, also known as "whiteheads."

    2. After hatching, the larvae feed by burrowing into the leaf sheath inside the stem. There are tiny holes, frass, and feces on the damaged plants' tissues. The larvae can switch internodes and are mobile.

    3. Since the plant produces additional tillers to offset the damage caused by larval feeding, symptoms may not appear during the vegetative stage. But doing so consumes energy, which eventually lowers yield.



    Organic Control:

    Pheromone bait traps can help you control the number of stem borers. The traps should be close to fences, especially those made of millet stems or other grasses and grain bins. Neem oil can be beneficial against Stem Borers when applied early in the growing season to infected plants. The "push-pull" strategy, which involves intercropping millet with crops like Desmodium, can be quite successful.
