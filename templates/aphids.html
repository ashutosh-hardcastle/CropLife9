    Pest Name: Aphids

    Information

    Aphids are small, soft-bodied insects with long legs and antennae. Depending on the species, they can be as little as 0.5 to 2mm in size and have bodies that are yellow, brown, red, or black. Their appearance vary from the often predominate wingless kinds to the winged, waxy, or woolly types.


    Symptoms:

    1. Usually, low to moderate populations don't harm crops. A bad infestation might make leaves and shoots curl, wilt, or turn yellow, which will limit the growth of the plant.

    2. There will be a general decrease in plant vigour. Aphids create honeydew, which can lead to a secondary fungus infection and result in mould growth on the leaves.

    3. Also drawn to the honeydew are ants. Aphids can persistently spread viruses from plant to plant, even in small quantities.



    Organic Control:

    Use an insecticidal soap solution or a solution based on plant oils, such as neem oil (3 ml/l), in cases of mild infestation. When it is humid, aphids are particularly vulnerable to fungus-related illnesses. Plants that are infected can also be removed using a water spray.
