    Pest Name: Soybean White-fly

    Information

    Whiteflies are sucking insects that feed on plant juices in both the immature and adult stages.With four whitish wings and a yellowish body, the mature stage is just around 1/16 inch long. The wings are held over the body in a roof-like fashion and are nearly parallel to the leaf surface. When disturbed, the adults will frequently fly up as you pass through a field. The undersides of leaves serve as food for the nymphs. They resemble scale insects because they are flattened.


    Symptoms:

    1. The plant's sap is sucked by both adults and nymphs, who then excrete honeydew onto the leaves, stems, and fruits as symptoms.

    2. The tissues that are damaged produce chlorotic patches and sooty molds. Except for the region along the veins, these patches may group and spread over the entire leaf during severe infections.

    3. Later on, the leaves may develop deformities, curl, or take on a cutting shape.



    Organic Control:

    Biological methods will differ depending on the crop and the particular species of whitefly involved. It is advised to use natural insecticides such as pyrethrins, insecticidal soaps, sugar-apple oil (Annona squamosal), Neem seed kernel extract (NSKE 5 percent), and Neem oil (five milliliters per liter of water). Beauveria bassiana, Isaria fumosorosea, Verticillium lecanii, and Paecilomyces fumosoroseus are examples of pathogenic fungi.
