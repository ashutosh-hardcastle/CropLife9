    Pest Name: Grasshopper

    Information

    Grasshoppers typically eat weeds and grasses but frequently move to cultivated crops. Their numbers fluctuate from year to year. With dry weather and high population densities, crop damage will probably be the most severe in years. When there is less natural vegetation, grasshoppers are forced to relocate to cultivated crops.


    Symptoms:

    1. The type of plant and the grasshopper's hunger affect the symptoms differently. Grasshoppers and locusts primarily consume leaves, causing damage to the margins or removing significant amounts of the leaf.

    2. The insect may also attack fruits, seed pods, and grains. They may even eat stems and bark if they are starving. Additionally, they chew on the shoots, which separates the reproductive components.



    Organic Control:

    Encouraging naturally occurring biological control agents like wasps, web-spinning spiders, parasitic flies and worms, ants, birds, and frogs is essential. To lower the population density of larvae, entomopathogenic fungi and Metarhizium acridum could potentially be utilised. Utilize homemade rice bran and saltwater poison baits.
